One Year

Hello, I hope you doing well
Today is actually a very special date. The day I saw something extraordinary in you. I don’t know whether you remember this date or not, but it’s significant to me. This day marks my liberation from my old self, the day when a single person changed me completely. Honestly, I don’t know why I love you—perhaps it's because I find every little thing about you captivating. But there is no rational explanation for these feelings.
As usual, I find myself constantly on the move, shuttling between cities for work. One week in one place, the next back in my apartment in Astana. We are writing a thesis and will have a defense soon. And graduation was set for June 28, at 10:00. It will take place at the Congress center.
A year has passed since I first met you. And you know what I said back then? I said that meeting you was like discovering a hidden treasure, a moment that I will always cherish. In this past year, every thought, every movement, every step I take, carries a piece of you with it. Your influence on my life is undeniable and profound.
As I navigate through the busyness of work and the pressures of academic life, thoughts of you bring me peace and joy. The memories are like beacons, guiding me through the hectic pace of life. I remember the day we met as if it were yesterday—the way your smile lit up the room, the sound of your laughter, and the warmth of your presence. These memories are in my heart, a constant reminder of the happiness you bring.
I wish I could convey just how much you mean to me, how deeply you have touched my life. But as Napoleon said, "If your absence means nothing to a person, then your presence would not change anything."
