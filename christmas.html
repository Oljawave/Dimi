07.01

Merry Christmas

I’d like to continue the letter I started before. And today is Christmas, so first, let me wish you a Merry Christmas. I wish you and your family all the best—good health to your mom and dad, and, of course, to you.
This year has been especially colorful for me, so unique and really amazing. Of course, there were disappointments, but they’re all in the past now. The most important thing in life is to take risks. After all, the biggest risk is staying in the same place year after year, without improving anything in your life.
I didn’t finish telling you what happened after my conversation with Mats. He told me he had a similar situation to mine with you. He said he tried everything he could, but it just didn’t work. Eventually, he gave up and accepted things as they were. But after some time, the girl called him herself, explained everything, and they ended up back together.
Then he said something that stuck with me: There’s no such thing as “the right person”—everyone can be the right person. It’s just about timing. Timing always messes things up. The right things happen at the right time, but only if people truly want them to.
At that moment, I ran back to my room to grab my phone. I opened WhatsApp and sat there, struggling with myself, staring at the damn call button—but I couldn’t do it. I just couldn’t... That’s when I realized that confidence isn’t enough when fear and feelings take over. Honestly, I’m not afraid of anything—except when it comes to you. With you, I’m a coward, the biggest coward.
It’s like a kettle—you want to touch it so badly, but you know you might get burned again.
The Embassy of Bulgaria is just a block away from my office, and every time I walk to work, I pass it. There’s a flag and the big letters BULGARIA. When I see it, I always remember the day I was planning to go there to find out all the details about entering the country. But then Velina wrote to me, saying you had a boyfriend, and it took everything in me to break my promise to come in November.
Winter in Astana is unbelievably beautiful, even if it’s freezing cold. All my friends say, “Wow, you went to a concert, you’ve been here and there,” and I always tell them this isn’t something to be proud of because it’s all just about paying for it. No, I’m not saying I’m rich—I’ve never been. But one dream remains: to take a walk with you through the streets of Astana.
Back in October, I started filming videos of Astana’s views for you. I’ll upload them soon; I’m almost done—had to reshoot everything from scratch.
I often go to the office just to avoid spending too much time alone in my apartment. I sit outside, or in winter, I go to Starbucks. It’s so warm there—if you know what I mean. And I imagine you sitting next to me.
So much time has passed, but you’re still in my heart. They say time heals all wounds, but I’m not sure about that. Yes, there were times when I didn’t think about you—thanks to the projects I worked on. They always saved me, along with music and movies. You won’t believe this, but I’ve listened to 110,000 minutes of music this year on Apple Music alone.
Sometimes, I think about how much has happened in a year and a half for both of us—so much that we wouldn’t have enough time, even another year and a half, to share it all with each other.
Merry Christmas, Dimana. Even though I’m a Muslim, haha, I made a wish: just one conversation with you. Even though I’m a coward.
