08.08

Salut

Hey yo, that's me again, hope u doing well
A lot of time has passed since I last wrote here. Life doesn't stand still, and many changes have occurred during this time. Recently, I was offered a job that I simply couldn't refuse. Now, I'm a front-end developer on a great project at Astana Hub. It's a big opportunity for me—something new, something I've been striving for. I haven't abandoned my business; I've made it autonomous so that it can operate without my constant involvement. Now I have the chance to focus on new challenges.
Looking back, I realized that up until this point, I've created 48 pages on this site. That's incredible, considering it's four times more than I've ever done for any other site. But you know how it is—sometimes even the most significant successes stay locked inside because there's no one to share them with. Well, I don't know, I'm writing everything here because I simply have no one to say anything to. I have practically no friends, I can't communicate with people, but with technology I'm like a family member.
I often walk around the city alone, especially at night. Nighttime Astana is something special—it's incredibly beautiful. When darkness covers the streets and the city lights shine, creating an atmosphere of mystery and tranquility, it feels like I'm in another world. It's my way of escaping reality, finding harmony amid the urban hustle. If I had one wish that could come true, I would wish to walk with you through Astana at night at least once in my life. On God...
Want to know how your site is being built? Imagine this: I work all day, then come home, grab something to eat, and watch videos about coding and history (like the one below). After that, I dive into some extra work, doing analytics for the store and getting everything in order. A week goes by, and I come home again. I throw my backpack on the bed, sit down at my computer, and write you a letter. I gather the videos I've filmed for you and roll out an update. but the strangest thing is that I first upload the video to YouTube and only then to the site with a link, it's easier that way. But the videos somehow get hundreds of views, which is scary. But anyways sounds cool right...
