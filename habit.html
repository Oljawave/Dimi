HABIT

Господи я не знаю, я не знаю, я просто не знаю, лучше бы сломались все 12 конечностей, либо лучше бы я был бессердечным

Най-необичайната ми навика

I would like to tell you about probably my most unusual habit. I think it will be interesting. I don't like to initiate conversations and interact with people, but few know that I love talking to homeless people. It all started from my childhood. From my early years, Дядо Сергей worked at our house. At that time, he was around 60 years old, and by nationality, he was Russian. Not surprising, as in our country, more than 130 nationalities live together. But I lived in a city with the least number of Kazakhs; mostly Russians, Koreans, Caucasians, Ukrainians, Tatars, Balkans, and many others lived there. I grew up among them, and I'm very glad about it.

So, Uncle Sergey lived in a small house on our territory. He was involved in construction, cleaning, and much more. In my childhood, I didn't pay much attention to him. But later, when I was around 8-10 years old, I spent a lot of time with him, asking various questions. By the time I was 11-13 years old, we were like best friends. He shared stories about his life, various anecdotes, and teachings. (Remember when I told you I tried my first cigarette at 13, guess with whom it was - Red Marlboro). Дядо Серьога was a wonderful person. Before losing everything, he was a successful businessman - he had a poultry farm, a family, a new Volga. But then, he lost everything. There are many stories about him. He always talked to me about humanity, time, and much more. What I remember is, "If you can see the beauty, it's because you carry beauty within yourself. Because the world is like a mirror, in which everyone sees their reflection." He was right. Since then, I started seeing only the beautiful in every person. Also, his legendary words: "Appreciate those who make your world brighter. True value is manifested in the ability to support each other, thus creating unique and valuable connections." Uncle Sergey left this world in 2019...

After that, on an intuitive level, without any reason, I started engaging in conversations with homeless people. There are very few of them, but they exist. It's very interesting to talk to them. They are not interested in politics, all the chaos that is happening around. And most importantly, they have retained humanity, that humor and wisdom, literacy and feelings, pure thoughts and love without any additives. People who don't judge by appearance, and it doesn't matter who you are, they just had bad luck in life.

In Ocean City, I also met one grandmother, in Starbucks on Asbury. She shared her life experience and such. I met her in Wawa, on the beach, etc. Perhaps you've seen her too (always with a bag, thin, white hair). Once, when I was gathering everything possible for that picnic with you, I decided to buy sushi. And guess what, she was passing by, she knew my name, as she often came to Starbucks (Andrew and I helped her as much as we could). So, I waited for my order, and seeing me, she came to talk. I told her about Asia, she about her youth. And as I was picking up my order, she looked at the sushi, said she had never eaten sushi. I decided to leave her one sushi set (that's why I brought only 12 pieces). And I also asked her for advice; she said, "Don't be shy about your feelings, don't try to count the stars, you might miss your moon..." And also, buy her flowers, no matter what kind, she shouted. And I, fool, didn't even ask where to buy them, collected everything and ran to you...

All of them wanted to give me valuable advice. They didn't tell me not to become like them; they told me not to become like society. Meeting such people, I always ask myself why God brings me together with people who are important to me, but I am not important to them... And this is not only about the homeless...

Using the advice of people who have learned to appreciate, love, express themselves, and see the beauty, I would like to say once again that you are the most beautiful person, a whole galaxy whom i love so much...
