28.08

One more time and for the last time

You will remain my sea breeze that fills my sails and leads me to endless horizons
Today is the day you flew home. That means I haven't seen you for exactly a year. I never thought I could miss someone for so long. There’s a saying: "Patience is sweet when you’re waiting for someone you love." But I never imagined it could be so sad. This year has been confusing for me. I lost many people, including you, Aldi, Daur, Bek, Temirlan, John, and others. And I gained no one, except for a love for shirts and white long socks. But somehow, I’m used to it—I’ve accepted it.
I spent the whole year working on the website. It turned out much better than I could have expected. It came out interesting, extensive, and colorful. I put a lot of time, feelings, hope, faith, and myself into it. Sometimes it feels like art, but other times it seems like an investment into nothing. All those 14,652 words that I wrote didn’t mean anything and turned out to be useless. The design and images ended up just being trash. In short, all 50 pages led me nowhere. Yes, I created 50 pages in a year, almost 15,000 words, and just as many lines of code. But I don’t regret it because it wasn’t pretense—it was done for you.
No, I’m not the guy you’d want to party with. I’m more occupied with something bigger than wasting time in clubs, bars, etc. I asked you to believe in me, told you that I would succeed. I got a job, I’m going to build a future. Since it's a joint startup, I have a flexible schedule. I’ll probably move to Asia with the job—maybe to Bali or Malaysia.
I can’t forget the moment when, sitting on the steps, I couldn’t take my eyes off you, or the moment when we sat at the picnic, by the church, by your door. It’s all like déjà vu. I’ll never forget how I learned new words in Bulgarian to surprise you, how I was mad at Velina for taking me away from you in Washington. How I ran to the pharmacy for ointment, how I came up with the idea to cut the fingers off the glove to bandage your wound. How I watched you through surveillance cameras. I’ll never forget the first hug, the hug when I gave you the cup, and the hug when I gave you the jacket, saying I didn’t want to lose you.
I look at other girls, and something isn’t right—they’re not you. I’m alone again. If I don’t think about it, everything’s okay. You know, when my mom was having surgery, I withdrew into myself and sometimes sat all night on Azburi. And one day, my neighbors—a Colombian and a Nigerian—found me. They came to support me, but they also said that you had a boyfriend or an ex, and that you were just playing with me. I refused to believe it because you told Aldi that you had no one, and you told me the same.
I want to say thank you for helping me meet people I still talk to. They are Timur (my only friend), Kay, Nellie, Nina, Sergio, Juan, and my friend Mark. Thank you for explaining to me that mutual love doesn’t always exist. Thank you for showing me that I’m capable of loving, even though I’ve never been loved myself. All the love I have is what my father never gave me.
When I asked to continue our relationship and what would happen to us, you said you needed to go home first, that you weren’t like everyone else, and that you would respond. I said I would wait. Now a year has passed, and I can wait longer, but I’m afraid there won’t be an answer. It seems I wasn’t even an option for you. It feels like everything ended, just like in the series "Sex Education."
And now August should let go of summer, just as I should let go of you. Shine every day, but without me. One more, last time: "Обичам те," I love you, even if you don’t love me. No, I’m not angry with you; you know I’m not one to blame someone for anything. I know I’m not needed by you, but I’ll always be open to you—I’ll always respond to you. If someone asks what pure feelings and faith in something bigger are, show them this website. You always said thank you and goodbye to me. Now it’s my turn: goodbye, Dimana...
