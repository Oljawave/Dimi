19.03

Я скучаю

Hello. Hope you are fine. I haven’t been here for a long time, it’s all because of the holidays in Kazakhstan. 10 days of ethnic celebration. One of them is National Dress and Music Day. We have a very rich collection of clothes. But I prefer my national costume. These are Puma sneakers, gray trousers, a watch, a zip-up jacket and of course my favorite backpack. I still hold back that style. I got it a long time ago. My friends even make fun of me for always carrying a backpack. Well, what can I do? I'm used to it. I remember in the USA I went out to meetings with you exactly like this. Now when I put on the same style of clothing, I feel as if I am going to meet you again. And music always plays in my ears. Without music it’s like I’m not me. I love any genre of music. From ethno to fonk. But there is also an oddity: I adore the classics. Sometimes I think that I am too unmodern. I was convinced of this when I was in Almaty and Tbilisi. I saw how young people were having fun. Everyone listens to aggressive music, drinks, smokes and does all sorts of crazy things. No, I don’t blame them, it just seems that I’m behind all this, maybe that’s why I’m not interesting. I’m more interested in walking, traveling, watching documentaries, old films, believing in love until the last breath, people’s requests, studying the unnecessary history of Carthage, listening to Ludovico Einaudi or Antonio Vivaldi. Talk about politics for hours and not drink a drop of alcohol. It sounds boring, but it is true. GYYYYYYYY. As they say, when you are happy you enjoy the music, and when you are upset you understand the lyrics. I remember even at Starbucks I turned on forgotten classics by John Numan, Calvin Harris, Sam Smith.
